\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We introduced \textbf{E-Deflare}, the first systematic framework to address the critical and overlooked problem of lens flare in event camera data. We established the theoretical foundation with a physics-based forward model of the non-linear flare corruption. This model guided the development of two core contributions: a physics-driven simulator that produced our large-scale \textbf{E-Flare2.7K} training set, and a novel, physically-principled acquisition system that captured \textbf{E-Flare-R}, the first paired real-world test set. Together with curated real-world sequences (\textbf{DSEC-Flare}), these form the \textbf{E-Deflare Benchmark}, a principled resource that enables our \textbf{E-DeflareNet} to achieve state-of-the-art performance and markedly improve downstream tasks. Beyond providing a robust de-flaring solution, our work offers a valuable blueprint for tackling other data-scarce challenges in event-based vision and beyond.

\noindent\textbf{Future Directions.} Adopting more powerful network architectures is expected to further improve background recovery in heavily flare-dominated regions. Collecting larger real-world paired event datasets will also enhance training data quality and improve model generalization and robustness.
